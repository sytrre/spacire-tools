    Understanding Insomnia Types ▼

    Sleep Onset Insomnia

    Difficulty falling asleep at the beginning of the night. If you consistently take more than 30 minutes to fall asleep, you may have sleep onset insomnia. This type is often associated with anxiety, racing thoughts, or poor sleep timing.

    Sleep Maintenance Insomnia

    Difficulty staying asleep throughout the night. You may wake multiple times or for extended periods. This type is often associated with stress, pain, sleep apnea, or environmental factors like noise or light.

    Early Morning Awakening

    Waking up earlier than desired (typically 2+ hours before intended) and being unable to fall back asleep. This type is often associated with depression, circadian rhythm issues, or aging.

    Mixed Insomnia

    A combination of two or more types. Many people experience difficulty both falling asleep and staying asleep. Mixed insomnia often indicates more complex underlying factors that may benefit from professional evaluation.

    About This Assessment ▼

    Clinical Foundation

    This quiz incorporates questions from the Insomnia Severity Index (ISI), a validated clinical tool developed by Dr. Charles Morin and widely used in sleep research and clinical practice.

    What We Assess

    • Severity of sleep onset, maintenance, and early awakening problems
    • Sleep satisfaction and quality of life impact
    • Sleep efficiency (actual sleep time vs. time in bed)
    • Contributing lifestyle and environmental factors

    Limitations

    This online assessment cannot diagnose sleep disorders or identify underlying medical conditions. Always consult a healthcare provider for persistent sleep issues.
